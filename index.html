Career Philosophy

A journey through law,
compliance & leadership

"Law is not merely a set of rules — it is the architecture within which organisations build trust, operate with integrity, and create sustainable value."

My legal career began in Mumbai, where I trained simultaneously across corporate law, company secretarial practice, and commercial litigation. Starting at Amines & Plasticizers and then ATCO Healthcare, I built the fundamentals: statutory compliance, board governance, SEBI filings, and courtroom representation across civil, criminal, and labour matters in courts throughout India. That grounding — rigorous, multi-disciplinary, and very much hands-on — shaped everything that followed.

Modern India Ltd deepened my experience in listed-company governance, cross-border M&A, and commercial contracting with international partners. At Wockhardt Ltd, I moved into genuinely complex transactional work: M&A due diligence spanning the UK, USA, Switzerland, and other European jurisdictions, IPO preparation for Wockhardt Hospitals, debt restructuring, and sophisticated joint venture structuring. It was at Wockhardt that I first operated at the intersection of legal rigour and strategic business decision-making.

Joining Fulford India Ltd (a Merck & Co. subsidiary) as Company Secretary and Legal Head was my entry into multinational pharmaceutical law. For nearly four years I managed India, Bangladesh, and Sri Lanka legal functions, supported the South-East Asia compliance hub, advised on FCPA compliance, and played a central role in the operational integration of Schering-Plough, Organon, and MSD businesses in India — one of the most complex pharmaceutical mergers of that era.

Fourteen years at Bristol-Myers Squibb — from Director of Legal in India to Senior Director heading legal and compliance across TIMER (Turkey, Israel, India, Middle East and Representative Markets) covering 50+ markets — became the defining chapter of my career. That breadth of geography, regulatory complexity, and commercial scale, combined with direct accountability to VP and Assistant General Counsel level, gave me a perspective on pharmaceutical law that few in-house practitioners acquire.

Throughout, I have invested in professional credentials that reflect genuine intellectual curiosity: dual Company Secretary qualifications across India and the UK, WIPO Advanced IP certifications, a Diploma in Cyber Law, Bar Council of India enrolment, and currently the SQE pathway to qualification as a Solicitor in England and Wales.
